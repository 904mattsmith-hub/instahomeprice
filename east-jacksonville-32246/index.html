The 32246 market: where East Jacksonville trades

ZIP 32246 sits at the center of everything east of the St. Johns — minutes from St. Johns Town Center, the corporate corridors along Butler Boulevard, and the bridges to the Beaches. That location keeps demand steady, but it also means values swing sharply from one subdivision to the next: an updated home near the Town Center loop and a same-size original-condition home two streets over can appraise thousands apart.

An instant estimate treats all of that as one averaged number. An RPR® valuation doesn't — it pulls the MLS comparables that actually match your home, and then I check them against what's really moving: renovation level, lot backing, and how your street feeds to Butler, Beach Boulevard, or Atlantic.

What moves values here

Priced street by street, not ZIP by ZIP

Town Center gravity

Proximity to St. Johns Town Center and the JTB corridor is the strongest single pull in 32246 — walkable-to-retail pockets and easy-commute streets carry a measurable premium.

Water & the Intracoastal

East toward 32225 and 32224, Intracoastal access, navigable canals, and marsh-front lots create an entirely different comp set. Water rights and dock condition matter as much as square footage.

Renovation spread

Much of East Jacksonville's housing stock was built in waves — the gap between updated and original-condition homes of the same floor plan is one of the widest in the city. The right comps make or break the number.
